About Anubhav

A grassroots non-profit collective of 25 dedicated members, based in Diamond Harbour, West Bengal, working to keep humanity alive in the most forgotten corners.

Est. Diamond Harbour

Our Story

Anubhav was born from a simple but powerful realisation: entire villages deep inside the forests of West Bengal and on remote Sundarbans islands were completely invisible to most relief and development efforts. Roads are broken or non-existent, mobile networks are weak, and without local guidance, outsiders cannot even find some of the hamlets.

Our team of 25 members, along with trusted local community guides, travels through jungles, river routes and difficult terrain to reach families who are otherwise left out. We provide food, clothing, winter blankets, and most importantly, educational support for children who deserve a fair chance at life.

Sundarbans Coaching Centre

Our flagship ongoing project — giving 70 children on a remote Sundarbans island the gift of education and hope for a better future.

Why This School Matters

Children in the Sundarbans face multiple challenges: long distances to formal schools, frequent flooding and cyclones, economic pressure on families, and a lack of safe study spaces.

Our coaching centre provides daily academic support, creating a consistent and caring learning environment. We work closely with parents and local leaders to keep education a priority, even when times are tough.

Where We Work

Our Reach

Four challenging districts in West Bengal where tribal and distressed communities live in remote, hard-to-access areas.

Jhargram

Dense forests and tribal hamlets far from main roads. Local guides help us navigate through jungle trails to reach families.

Purulia

Hilly, arid terrain with scattered tribal settlements. One of the most backward districts in West Bengal with extreme summer and winter conditions.

Bankura

Rural interior villages surrounded by forests. Communities here face poverty, limited healthcare and educational opportunities.

Sundarbans

Remote river islands accessible only by boat. Cyclones, flooding and tiger threats make life incredibly challenging. Home to our coaching centre for 70 children.

From the Field

Stories of Hope

In a small hamlet deep inside the forest in Jhargram, an elderly widow lived alone in a mud house with a leaking roof and no warm bedding. Every winter, she would sit awake most of the night, unable to sleep in the cold. With the support of our donors, we were able to bring her a thick blanket and some basic food essentials. When our team visited her a month later, she told us, "This is the first winter in many years when I have slept peacefully."

Field visit — Jhargram

A young girl in our Sundarbans coaching centre was on the verge of dropping out because her family couldn't afford her school supplies. Through our sponsorship program, she received books, stationery and daily tuition support. Today she continues to attend school with confidence and dreams of becoming a teacher one day.
